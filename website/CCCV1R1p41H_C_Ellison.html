CAMPBELL COUSINS CORRESPONDENCE




38 Garden Street,
Canandaigua, N.Y..
  June 20, 1923.

Dear Cousins:-

This being my first report, I hardly know what to write.

As to our health, it is fine at this time and I guess that [my sister.] Edna [ELLISON Wright] and her family are in good health although I have not heard from them in nearly six months so cannot state definitely. Father [William] Ellison is looking fine and is in very good health.

We have had an increase in our family since we last attended the Campbell Reunion. Thelma [living] was born September 30, 1920.

[Sister] Virginia [later Cornell] apparently is entirely over her trouble of Tubercular Glands and is feeling fine again.

I cannot say much about Edna and family as I do not know.

I am still working for the N.Y.C. R.R. Co. as Agent at Victor, N. Y. and am living in Canandaigua, N. Y.

Possibly after reading letters in your first report, I will be able to give you a little better letter for the next or September report.

Yours very truly, .

COUSIN H. C. ELLISON

- Report 1 - Page 41 -
